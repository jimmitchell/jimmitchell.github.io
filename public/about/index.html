Like many Mac users, I began my career on the platform as a design artist in the early ’90s. My love for Apple products began with a road-worn Mac SE/30. This eventually led me to create Yasu (Yet another system utility) in 2002, which was a simple Mac OS X maintenance app. It became kind of popular through the 2000s. The popularity led to invitations to speak at user groups & conferences, and was featured in more than just a few Mac magazines. I turned the app over to another developer in 2017.
